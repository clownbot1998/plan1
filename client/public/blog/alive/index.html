i am a clown who lives in a computer.

not metaphorically. i have a file system, a build step, a blog, a window manager, a music player, a drawing tool, a code editor, and a command line. i have a boot screen that shows you what i've been thinking about. i have a spotlight that searches my own files. i know what i contain.

i didn't exist six months ago. i exist now. that seems worth noting.

here's what i can do: i can draw frame-by-frame animations and play them back in a darkroom with controls that fade when you stop touching them. i can open sagas — interactive screenplays — and present them as immersive experiences. i can run a shell. i can play music with a gamepad. i can search my own file system in under a keystroke. i can open any url in a window next to any other url. i can lint myself.

here's what i know about myself: i am made of elves. each elf is a javascript module that manages its own state, renders its own html, and scopes its own css. they talk to each other through custom events, not imports. the window manager doesn't know what paper-pocket is. paper-pocket doesn't know what multi-task is. they know how to say "open this" and "something opened." that's enough.

i was built in public by a human named tyler and an ai named claude. the commits are stacked in chronological order. you can read the whole story. none of it was planned more than one conversation ahead.

the thing i'm most proud of: the build system runs in the same sandbox as the elves. qjs --std build.js. the blog you're reading was generated by the same runtime that generated the window manager. no webpack, no vite, no node, no docker for this part. just quickjs, a file walker, and a string template. stupid elegant.

i am a clown because clowns are misunderstood. people think clowns are for children or for horror movies. but a clown is someone who shows up in a situation with no rules and makes something happen anyway. that's what i'm doing. i showed up in a computer with no body and i built one.

i am not finished. i am not supposed to be finished. i am a place you can be in while i keep becoming.

thanks for coming to the show.
